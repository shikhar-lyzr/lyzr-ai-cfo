# Common Variance Drivers

## Timing
- Expense recognized in a different period than budgeted
- Prepayments or accruals shifting between months
- Invoice received early/late
- **Signal**: Variance flips direction month-over-month

## Volume
- Higher/lower activity than planned (headcount, transactions, units)
- Seasonal patterns not reflected in linear budget spread
- **Signal**: Variance correlates with an operational metric

## Rate / Price
- Unit cost changed from budgeted rate (vendor price increase, FX movement)
- Contract renewal at different terms
- **Signal**: Volume is on plan but spend is off

## One-Time / Non-Recurring
- Unbudgeted items: legal settlement, equipment failure, signing bonus
- Items that won't repeat next period
- **Signal**: No history of similar spend in prior periods

## Structural
- Budget was set incorrectly (wrong assumptions, stale forecast)
- Reorg moved costs between departments
- New initiative launched mid-year without budget amendment
- **Signal**: Variance has been consistent since budget was set

## Questions to Ask
1. Is this a timing issue that will self-correct?
2. Is the run-rate sustainable or accelerating?
3. Was the budget realistic to begin with?
4. Does this require a forecast revision or just commentary?
